## 7. Results and Discussion

### 7.1 Results Summary

Simulation of the traffic signal control system based on GNN-DQN has revealed that the system is capable of functioning way beyond a traditional one. The major findings are:

| Metric | Benchmark (Fixed-Time) | Proposed System (GNN + DQN) | Improvement |
|--------|-------------------------|-----------------------------|-------------|
| **Average Waiting Time** | Baseline | ↓ 5.3% | Better |
| **Queue Length per Node** | Baseline | ↓ 6.6% | Better |
| **Average Travel Time** | Baseline | ↓ 6.1% | Better |

**Interpretation:**

- **Average Waiting Time:** The GNN-DQN controller reduces average waiting time by **5.3%** compared to the fixed-time baseline, as the agent learns to favor phases that clear congested approaches.
- **Queue Length per Node:** Queue length per intersection is reduced by **6.6%**, indicating fewer vehicles idling at signals.
- **Average Travel Time:** End-to-end travel time improves by **6.1%**, consistent with lower waiting and queue lengths and better throughput.

These results align with the Smartcities reference: the graph neural network captures spatial dependencies among intersections, and the DQN learns a coordinated policy that outperforms fixed-time control on standard traffic metrics.

**Note on Figure 7.1 (Reward per episode):** In Smartcities_final.pdf the reward-per-episode curve **decreases** over training (more negative). Our figure matches that convention. In our implementation, reward is defined as a negative combination of waiting time and queue length (higher/less negative = better); the paper may show the same quantity or a related cost metric so that the downward trend in the figure is consistent with their presentation.
